Open Source Sharing

TANDEM was built by incorporating existing Open Source programs within a Python and Django framework. If you want to learn more about how TANDEM was built -- or if you want to use any of our code -- please review the information provided below.

TANDEM encourages its users continued explorations into the digital world, providing that the Terms & Licensing are followed, as documented below.

User-Generated Content

  • All data outputs produced by TANDEM belong to you, and only you can determine how to distribute your own data.
  • Any final research made with a TANDEM data output is yours and yours alone (be we encourage you to look into Open Access and Creative Commons Licenses).
  • If you decide to share your data for the TANDEM website, your dataset(s) would be shared with the Creative Commons Universal 1.0 Public Domain Dedication (CC 0).

Source Material

  • Because the functionality of TANDEM relies on user-generated content, we require that you be responsible for obtaining necessary permissions for the image content uploaded to the platform.
  • We at TANDEM are NOT lawyers, but here's our best advice for source material:
    • If you are interested in obtaining source material in the public domain, consider searching for content in the public domain on HathiTrust.
    • Obtaining source content in the public domain will allow you the ability to freely publish or advertise your source material and any datasets.
    • Content not in the public domain will require obtaining permissions before distributing or publishing your material.
